Rider Account Deletion

Passenger & Rider Account Deletion Request Portal
The AT DRIVE application provides riders with full transparency and control over their data, allowing them to permanently delete their account and associated personal data in strict accordance with Google Play Store Data Safety requirements and user protection rules.
Important Notice:
Once your account is deleted, the process cannot be undone. All your saved profile history, promotional benefits, and wallet balances will be permanently destroyed. Future use of AT DRIVE will require creating an entirely new rider account.

When Will Your Data Be Deleted?

Account deletion requests are initiated instantly. All personal data queues are processed, purged, and completely sanitized from our operational servers within a timeframe not exceeding 30 days from the date the request is submitted. In most standard scenarios, the erasure occurs much faster.

How to Delete Your Account from Within the App

The standard and most streamlined method to wipe your account is from within the application interface:

  1. Open the AT DRIVE Rider application on your device.
  2. Go to the Settings or Profile Management menu.
  3. Select the option "Delete Account and Data".
  4. Review the final warning prompt regarding data loss.
  5. Confirm your permanent deletion request.

Data That Will Be Permanently Deleted

Upon successful verification of your request, the following user categories will be permanently deleted from our live databases:

  • Full legal name, registered mobile phone number, and account email address.
  • Profile picture, avatars, and essential account configuration metadata.
  • Saved addresses, custom pinned locations, and favorite historical routes.
  • Granular trip logs, transaction history, and historical geographic routes associated with the account.
  • Device tokens used for Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) notifications.

Data We May Be Obligated to Retain Temporarily

Under legitimate regulatory, financial, and safety policies, certain limited invoices, transactional records, or active disputes cannot be dropped instantly. This data is kept strictly to comply with local corporate legal frameworks, financial audit standards, preventing platform abuse, or settling outstanding wallet disputes.
